Section 1

Institutional Overview

Mission Statement

AI Trade School exists to equip working adults and career-changers with the applied skills, professional judgment, and ethical foundations necessary to deploy, manage, document, and operate artificial intelligence systems in organizational settings. Through a rigorous, competency-based curriculum grounded in workforce development principles, the institution prepares learners for immediate productive employment as AI Operators across public, private, and nonprofit sectors.

Vision Statement

AI Trade School envisions a workforce in which AI literacy is as fundamental as digital literacy, and in which trained AI Operators serve as essential contributors to organizational efficiency, innovation, and responsible technology adoption. The institution aspires to be the preeminent non-degree workforce credential for applied AI operations, recognized by employers, workforce development boards, and industry partners as a standard of professional competence.

Educational Philosophy

The institution’s instructional design is guided by four core principles:

  1. Applied Competence Over Theoretical Abstraction. All instruction centers on real-world tasks, tools, and decision-making scenarios that learners will encounter in professional settings. Theory is introduced only to the extent it informs applied practice.
  2. Demonstration-Based Mastery. Students prove competence through portfolio artifacts, applied projects, and performance assessments rather than passive examination. Evidence of ability is the standard of achievement.
  3. Ethical Responsibility as a Core Competency. Responsible AI use is not an elective consideration; it is integrated into every course and every assessment. Graduates are expected to operate AI systems with awareness of bias, privacy, transparency, and organizational accountability.
  4. Accessible, Stackable Credentialing. The program begins with free foundational courses, ensuring that financial barriers do not prevent entry. Credentials are stackable, providing clear and incremental pathways to professional recognition.

Workforce & Trade-School Model

AI Trade School operates under a workforce and trade-school educational model. The institution:

  • Does not grant academic degrees; it awards professional certificates and certifications aligned with employer expectations
  • Designs curriculum to meet documented employer needs and workforce development frameworks
  • Emphasizes job-readiness, applied skill demonstration, and portfolio development over academic credit accumulation
  • Structures clock hours and competency standards in alignment with continuing education norms
  • Serves adult learners, career-changers, incumbent workers, and organizational cohorts seeking practical AI capability

Program Structure & Credential Pathway

The AI Trade School program is organized into three progressive tiers. Each tier builds on the competencies established in the previous tier, creating a clear pathway from foundational awareness to professional certification.

Three-Tier Model

  • Tier 1: Foundational Courses (Free, Non-Credit) — AI Literacy Certificate
  • Tier 2: Core Applied Courses (Paid) — Applied AI Practitioner Certificate
  • Tier 3: CAIO Certification (Certification Fee) — Certified AI Operator
Tier Credential Cost Key Milestone
Tier 1 AI Literacy Certificate Free Completion of 5 foundational courses with passing assessments
Tier 2 Applied AI Practitioner Certificate $149–$297 per course Completion of 5 applied courses with portfolio artifacts
Tier 3 Certified AI Operator (CAIO) Certification fee Capstone project, written exam, oral defense

Learner Entry Points

All learners begin at Tier 1 regardless of prior experience. There is no advanced placement or credit-for-experience pathway. This ensures all students share a common foundation in AI concepts, tools, ethics, and professional terminology before advancing to applied coursework.

Workforce Alignment

  • Tier 1 prepares learners for AI awareness roles and general AI literacy within any professional context
  • Tier 2 prepares learners for applied AI operations roles, including prompt engineering, workflow automation, and business AI integration
  • Tier 3 prepares learners for autonomous AI Operator positions with organizational accountability

Foundational Courses (Tier 1)

Tier 1 comprises five foundational courses totaling approximately 46 clock hours. All foundational courses are free and open to all enrolled learners. Completion of all five courses with passing assessments qualifies the learner for the AI Literacy Certificate.

AI 101 — Foundations of Artificial Intelligence
10 Hours Tier 1

Learning Objectives

  1. Define artificial intelligence, machine learning, and large language models in professional context
  2. Distinguish between narrow AI, general AI, and generative AI capabilities and limitations
  3. Explain the basic architecture and training process of large language models
  4. Identify at least five industry-specific AI use cases across different sectors
  5. Articulate the distinction between AI as a tool and AI as an autonomous agent

Assessment

  • Multiple-choice knowledge assessment (75% passing threshold)
  • Short-answer reflection on AI applications in the learner’s professional context

Outcome: Foundational knowledge sufficient for informed professional conversation about AI capabilities, limitations, and applications.

ChatGPT Fundamentals — Human–AI Interaction
8 Hours Tier 1

Learning Objectives

  1. Compose effective prompts using structured techniques (role, context, format, constraints)
  2. Apply iterative prompt refinement to improve output quality across task types
  3. Identify common failure modes including hallucination, bias, and context window limitations
  4. Use ChatGPT productively for at least three professional task categories
  5. Evaluate AI-generated outputs for accuracy, completeness, and appropriateness

Assessment

  • Prompt portfolio demonstrating competence across three distinct task categories
  • Knowledge assessment on limitations and failure modes (75% passing threshold)

Outcome: Competent ChatGPT use with understanding of reliability boundaries and output evaluation.

Overview of AI Tools and Platforms
10 Hours Tier 1

Learning Objectives

  1. Identify and describe the function of 15+ AI tools across major categories
  2. Apply structured evaluation criteria to assess tool suitability for specific tasks
  3. Distinguish between tool categories: generative, analytical, automation, and integration
  4. Demonstrate basic proficiency in at least three AI tools beyond ChatGPT
  5. Create a personal AI tool inventory with use-case documentation

Assessment

  • Tool Evaluation Matrix covering five tools with structured criteria
  • Knowledge assessment (75% passing threshold)

Outcome: Informed tool selection capability for professional tasks and organizational contexts.

AI for Personal and Professional Productivity
8 Hours Tier 1

Learning Objectives

  1. Conduct a personal workflow audit to identify AI-augmentable tasks
  2. Implement AI tools for at least five distinct productivity use cases
  3. Measure and document productivity improvements from AI integration
  4. Design a repeatable AI-augmented productivity system for ongoing use
  5. Identify tasks where AI use is inappropriate or counterproductive

Assessment

  • Productivity Audit with before/after demonstration
  • Knowledge assessment (75% passing threshold)

Outcome: Systematic AI integration into daily professional work with quantified productivity gains.

AI Careers, Ethics, and Workforce Readiness
10 Hours Tier 1

Learning Objectives

  1. Identify at least 10 AI-related professional roles and their requirements
  2. Describe the scope, responsibilities, and career trajectory of the AI Operator role
  3. Apply ethical frameworks to AI deployment scenarios including transparency, fairness, and accountability
  4. Identify sources of AI bias and describe mitigation strategies
  5. Articulate data privacy considerations relevant to AI tool use in organizational settings
  6. Develop a personal career development plan incorporating AI skills

Assessment

  • Ethical Case Analysis covering two scenarios with structured reasoning
  • Career Development Plan with skills inventory and growth targets
  • Knowledge assessment (75% passing threshold)

Outcome: Professional landscape understanding, ethical reasoning skills, and actionable career strategy.

Certified AI Operator (CAIO) Program

Purpose

The Certified AI Operator (CAIO) designation is the capstone professional credential of AI Trade School. It fills a critical gap in the workforce: trained professionals who manage, deploy, and operate AI systems within organizations — distinct from those who build or research AI technology.

Professional Scope — The AI Operator

The AI Operator is a professional role defined by the following competencies:

  • Deploys AI tools and workflows within organizational settings
  • Manages ongoing AI operations including monitoring, troubleshooting, and optimization
  • Documents AI systems for knowledge management, compliance, and continuity
  • Applies ethical reasoning and risk assessment to AI deployment decisions
  • Communicates AI capabilities, limitations, and outcomes to stakeholders at all levels
  • Evaluates and selects AI tools and vendors based on organizational requirements
  • Designs AI-augmented workflows that integrate with existing business processes
  • Exercises professional judgment on AI outputs, recognizing when human intervention is required
Note

The AI Operator role is distinct from AI Engineer, Data Scientist, and IT Administrator. The AI Operator occupies the critical operational layer between AI technology and organizational function.

Prerequisites

  • AI Literacy Certificate (Tier 1)
  • Applied AI Practitioner Certificate (Tier 2)
  • Complete professional portfolio meeting all competency standards

Time Commitment

The CAIO certification phase requires approximately 40–60 additional hours of work, including capstone development, examination preparation, and oral defense. Candidates have a maximum of 12 months to complete all certification requirements from the date of eligibility.

Total program hours across all three tiers: approximately 186–231 clock hours.

Instructional Delivery Model

Asynchronous Instruction

All instruction is delivered asynchronously. There are no live sessions or scheduled class meetings. Instructional delivery includes:

  • Pre-recorded video demonstrations and lectures
  • Written instructional modules and reference materials
  • Structured project briefs and assignment specifications
  • Discussion forums for peer interaction and knowledge sharing
  • Instructor feedback on submitted work and portfolio artifacts
  • Self-paced progress through course materials

Adult Learner Model (Andragogy)

AI Trade School’s instructional design follows principles of adult learning theory:

  • Relevance: All content is directly tied to professional application
  • Autonomy: Learners control their own pace and schedule
  • Experience Integration: Prior professional experience informs learning and project work
  • Problem-Centered: Instruction is organized around real-world problems, not abstract topics
  • Intrinsic Motivation: Career advancement and skill development drive engagement

Demonstration-Based Teaching Model

Each instructional unit follows a five-step demonstration-based model:

  1. Context Setting: Establish the professional scenario and learning objective
  2. Demonstration: Instructor models the skill or workflow in a real-world context
  3. Guided Practice: Learner replicates the demonstrated skill with structured support
  4. Independent Application: Learner applies the skill to a novel scenario
  5. Reflection and Feedback: Learner receives feedback and reflects on the process

Portfolio-Driven Learning

Portfolio development serves four functions within the program:

  • Evidence of Competence: Demonstrates applied skill to assessors and employers
  • Employment Preparation: Provides concrete work samples for professional use
  • Reflective Practice: Encourages ongoing self-assessment and improvement
  • Assessment Basis: Serves as the primary evaluation mechanism for Tier 2 courses

Assessment & Evaluation

Competency-Based Assessment

All assessment at AI Trade School is competency-based. Students are evaluated against defined competency standards, not against one another. There is no curved grading, no class ranking, and no bell-curve distribution. The question is not how a student compares to peers, but whether the student has demonstrated the required competency.

Grading Model

Tier Assessment Type Grading Model Minimum Standard
Tier 1 Knowledge assessments Percentage-based 75%
Tier 2 Applied projects & portfolio Rubric (Competent / Not Yet Competent) Competent on all criteria
CAIO Capstone Capstone project Rubric-based Competent on all criteria
CAIO Written Exam Written examination Percentage-based 80% overall, 70% per domain
CAIO Oral Defense Oral defense Rubric (Pass / Not Yet Passed) Pass on all evaluation criteria

Rubric Evaluation Standards

  • Competent: The student has demonstrated the required knowledge, skill, or ability at a level consistent with professional practice. The work product meets all specified criteria and demonstrates applied understanding.
  • Not Yet Competent: The student has not yet demonstrated the required competency. Specific feedback is provided to guide improvement. The student may resubmit within the reassessment policy.

Reassessment Policy

Assessment Reassessment Allowance Conditions
Tier 1 Knowledge Assessments Up to 3 retakes 48-hour waiting period between attempts
Tier 2 Portfolio Artifacts Up to 2 revisions Revised work must address all feedback
CAIO Written Examination 1 retake 30-day waiting period
CAIO Oral Defense 1 reattempt 30-day waiting period; revised capstone required

Certification Standards

CAIO Competency Domains

The Certified AI Operator examination and capstone project assess competence across six professional domains:

  1. AI Tool Proficiency
  2. Prompt Engineering & Optimization
  3. Workflow Automation & Integration
  4. Business Operations & Strategy
  5. Documentation & Governance
  6. Ethics, Risk & Professional Judgment

Competency Matrix

Domain Key Skills Assessment Method Standard
AI Tool Proficiency Tool selection, configuration, deployment Capstone + Written Competent / 80%
Prompt Engineering Design, optimization, documentation Capstone + Written Competent / 80%
Workflow Automation Mapping, architecture, deployment Capstone + Written Competent / 80%
Business Operations Strategy, implementation, ROI Capstone + Written Competent / 80%
Documentation & Governance System docs, compliance, knowledge mgmt Capstone + Oral Competent / Pass
Ethics & Professional Judgment Risk assessment, bias, accountability Written + Oral 80% / Pass

Capstone Requirements

The CAIO capstone project requires six components:

  1. Organizational Context Analysis: Assessment of the target organization’s AI readiness, needs, and constraints
  2. AI System Design: Architecture and design of an AI system addressing identified organizational needs
  3. Implementation Documentation: Complete documentation of system deployment, configuration, and operation
  4. Ethical and Risk Assessment: Analysis of ethical considerations, risks, and mitigation strategies
  5. Impact Evaluation: Measurement and analysis of system impact on organizational outcomes
  6. Stakeholder Communication Materials: Professional-grade materials for presenting the system to organizational stakeholders

Written Examination

The CAIO written examination comprises 100 questions across the six competency domains:

  • 60 multiple-choice questions
  • 20 short-answer questions
  • 20 scenario-based questions

Time allowed: 3 hours. Administered online with proctoring. Passing standard: 80% overall with a minimum of 70% in each domain.

Oral Defense

Component Duration Evaluation Focus
Project Presentation 20 minutes Clarity, completeness, professional communication
Panel Questions 20 minutes Technical depth, decision rationale, adaptability
Ethical Scenario 10 minutes Ethical reasoning, risk assessment, judgment

Code of Conduct & Ethics

Ethical AI Use Standards

  1. Use AI tools responsibly, with awareness of their limitations and potential for harm
  2. Disclose AI-generated content when required by professional, organizational, or legal standards
  3. Avoid using AI systems to deceive, manipulate, or cause harm to individuals or organizations
  4. Report identified biases, errors, or ethical concerns in AI outputs and systems
  5. Protect data privacy and confidentiality when using AI tools with organizational or personal information

Academic Integrity

  1. Submit only original work unless explicitly permitted to collaborate
  2. Properly attribute all sources, tools, and AI assistance used in assignments
  3. Do not misrepresent AI-generated work as entirely human-produced
  4. Do not share assessment content, answers, or examination materials with other students

Professional Conduct

  1. Engage respectfully with instructors, peers, and staff in all communications
  2. Provide constructive feedback and participate in learning communities professionally
  3. Respect intellectual property rights and licensing agreements for all tools and content
  4. Maintain confidentiality of organizational information used in coursework and projects
  5. Represent credentials and qualifications accurately and honestly

Enforcement Process

  1. Report: Concern submitted through the conduct reporting portal
  2. Investigation: Review of evidence and circumstances by administration
  3. Notification: Student notified of the concern and given opportunity to respond
  4. Determination: Finding of responsibility or no responsibility
  5. Sanctions: Range from warning to credential revocation, depending on severity
  6. Appeal: Students may appeal determinations within 14 calendar days

Accessibility & Inclusion

Commitment

AI Trade School is committed to meeting WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility standards across all learning materials and digital platforms.

Accommodations Policy

The following accommodations are available upon request:

  1. Extended time on timed assessments
  2. Alternative format materials (audio, large print, screen-reader compatible)
  3. Modified assessment delivery methods
  4. Assistive technology compatibility support
  5. Communication preference accommodations
  6. Flexible deadline arrangements for documented needs

Universal Design for Learning (UDL)

AI Trade School applies UDL principles across six areas:

  • Multiple Means of Representation: Content delivered in text, video, audio, and visual formats
  • Multiple Means of Engagement: Varied activities, self-paced progress, and choice in project topics
  • Multiple Means of Action and Expression: Diverse assessment formats and portfolio options
  • Culturally Responsive Design: Inclusive examples, diverse perspectives, and global applicability
  • Plain Language: Clear, jargon-free instructional writing accessible to non-native English speakers
  • Captioning and Transcription: All video content captioned; transcripts available for all media
